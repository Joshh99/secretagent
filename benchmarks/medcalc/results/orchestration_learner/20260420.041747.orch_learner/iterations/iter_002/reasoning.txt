1. What SPECIFIC failure pattern did you identify?
Scoring calculators (like CHA2DS2-VASc, APACHE II, Centor) occasionally misinterpret the absence of a condition or over-infer points, leading to slight off-by-one errors in risk scores.
2. How many of the shown failures share this pattern?
Several scoring cases (CHA2DS2-VASc, Centor, PERC, Wells, GBS) show 1-2 point absolute errors due to hallucinated or overly aggressive condition inference.
3. What is the MINIMAL change that fixes this pattern?
Add a strong negative constraint to the `analyze_scoring_conditions` docstring to explicitly state: "DO NOT assume conditions are present unless explicitly mentioned or strongly implied. If uncertain, mark as absent."
4. Why will this change NOT break currently working cases?
It only applies to the reasoning stage for scoring calculators and acts as a conservative regularizer against hallucination. It does not touch Python logic or alter extraction rules for labs/measurements.
