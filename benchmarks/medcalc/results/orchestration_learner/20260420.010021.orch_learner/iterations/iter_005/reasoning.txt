1. What SPECIFIC failure pattern did you identify?
In `workflow()`, when `identify_calculator()` returns a dictionary, the fuzzy string matching for the calculator name can sometimes match an incorrect calculator if the predicted name is a short substring of multiple calculators, or it might fail to find a match and leave `calc_name = None`. When `calc_name` is wrongly assigned, the downstream extraction asks for the wrong fields, causing validation to fail or extract garbage, and ultimately forces a fallback to the LLM. 
2. How many of the shown failures share this pattern?
Several date/pregnancy-related calculations (e.g. Estimated Due Date, Estimated of Conception) show the extraction stage asking for `gestational_weeks` instead of the date parameters, meaning they were routed to the wrong calculator signature during fuzzy matching.
3. What is the MINIMAL change that fixes this pattern?
Enhance the fuzzy matching in `workflow()` to prefer exact matches (case-insensitive) first before falling back to substring matching. This prevents "Estimated Due Date" from being incorrectly matched to another calculator just because of a partial string overlap.
4. Why will this change NOT break currently working cases?
It only improves the accuracy of mapping the LLM-identified calculator name to the exact keys in the `signatures` registry. Exact matches are prioritized, which is strictly safer than immediate substring matching.
